# ARP Adapter & Multi-Agent Register Discipline: An Evidence Review

## 1. Verdict

A model-agnostic register/adapter convention does **not** port cleanly across providers — this is the single best-supported finding in the dossier, and it is the strongest empirical case for ARP's adapter layer. Optimized prompts, output-format conventions, and instruction-following reliability all degrade family-to-family by large, measured margins (10.77pp on HumanEval transfer, up to 76 accuracy points from format alone, 18.3%–61.8% reliability declines under paraphrase). The historical agent-communication-language record (KQML, FIPA-ACL/Arcol) adds a sharp design constraint: those standards failed not because they lacked structure but because their semantics were *mentalistic* — compliance was defined over the sender's private beliefs and could not be verified by a third party. The lesson is that ARP's register discipline must be **publicly checkable**, not defined over hidden state. On the question of whether shared structured-state compression actually helps multi-agent cost or reliability, the evidence splits cleanly: structured/typed **handoff discipline** measurably helps (ReWOO ~5x token cut, MetaGPT structured artifacts, blackboard state-sharing, AgentAsk clarification at handoffs), but a **designed shared compressed IR/register language, isolated against no-IR at swarm scale, is unproven** — no located source runs that ablation, and the strong swarm numbers come from adjacent levers (learned NL compression, sparsification, transport choice, shared logs). That null result is itself the honest finding for ARP/AIL-0's most ambitious claim.

## 2. Synthesis and Design Recommendation

The portability evidence is unambiguous and convergent across four independent studies. A prompt tuned for one model carries a large penalty to another: PromptBridge measures the GPT-5–optimal HumanEval prompt at 99.39% on GPT-5 but only 68.70% transferred to Llama-3.1-70B-Instruct, against Llama's own optimum of 79.47% — a 10.77pp drop the paper names "model drifting" [PromptBridge]. Sclar et al. show the same at the *format* level: semantically-equivalent changes in delimiters, casing, and spacing produce up to 76 accuracy points of spread, and crucially "format performance only weakly correlates between models" [FormatSpread]. PromptPort reproduces this for output conventions across six open-weight families with 0.4–0.6 F1 portability gaps [PromptPort], and IFEval++ shows instruction-following reliability under convention-preserving paraphrase declining 18.3% (GPT-5) to 61.8% (Qwen3-0.6B) [IFEval++]. The design implication — that ARP needs a *per-target adapter* rather than one canonical convention — is sound, but it is the strand author's inference: none of these papers mention ARP. What is measured is the portability gap; the adapter is the engineering response to it.

This points to a layered design. ARP/AIL-0 should define a **stable internal register schema** (the canonical state representation agents reason over) and push all model-specific variance into a thin **adapter** that renders that schema into the prompt/format convention each target tolerates and canonicalizes/repairs the target's output back into the schema. PromptPort's result is the direct template: format-recovery alone buys +6–8 F1 and a verifier-driven repair step adds +14–16 F1, reaching 99.3% of zero-shot oracle [PromptPort]. The adapter is not just an emitter; it is an emitter *plus* a validating parser, because output conventions collapse differently across families and weak models emit fenced or malformed structure that needs per-model repair.

The KQML/FIPA-ACL history sharpens *what* the register discipline should encode. Singh's 1998 analysis is the load-bearing source: speech-act ACLs failed as interoperability standards because "both Arcol and KQML emphasize the private perspective... concerned only with the sender's perspective," so "if you cannot determine compliance, the standard is useless," and "for compliance to be testable the ACL's semantics must have a public perspective" [Singh1998]. KQML further fragmented into incompatible dialects, so "nonproprietary agents cannot interoperate" even where ACLs were used inside proprietary systems [Singh1998]. For ARP this is a two-part directive: (i) register semantics must be defined over *observable, third-party-checkable* artifacts — what was written to a shared state, not what an agent believes — and (ii) the schema must be tight enough to prevent dialect drift, the failure mode that actually killed KQML in practice. Singh's structural point that ACL primitives covered only assertives/directives, omitting first-class commissives and declaratives [Singh1998], also argues for an explicit, complete act/intent vocabulary rather than an open-ended one that gets simulated inconsistently.

Modern frameworks confirm that *structured handoffs* — the conservative, well-evidenced half of register discipline — measurably help. MetaGPT constrains inter-agent messages to role-specific structured outputs (PRDs, design docs, interface specs) over a shared publish-subscribe pool rather than free dialogue, reaching 85.9% Pass@1 on HumanEval and 87.7% on MBPP [MetaGPT]. ReWOO's register split — separating a reasoning channel (Planner) from observation/tool-output channels (Worker/Solver) so tool outputs are not re-fed each step — is the cleanest causal result: ~5x token reduction (9,795 → 1,986 measured tokens) plus a small accuracy gain on HotpotQA [ReWOO]. AgentAsk audited 824 multi-agent logs and found inter-agent handoffs dominated by a small set of structured failure modes (Signal Corruption 36.8%, Data Gap 29.1%), then showed a lightweight clarification controller at handoffs recovers most of a heavy GPT-5 evaluator's gain at <5% latency/token overhead [AgentAsk]. These results justify ARP investing in validated, schema-typed handoff packets.

But the dossier is honest about the ceiling. MAST (1,642 annotated traces, 7 frameworks) concludes that structuring messages with schemas/protocols is *not by itself sufficient* for inter-agent misalignment — those failures "demand deeper social reasoning" — and that targeted structural fixes yield only modest gains (ChatDev CEO-final-say change: +9.4% task success), with robust reliability "often requir[ing] more than isolated fixes... fundamental MAS redesigns" [MAST]. And on multi-agent *state compression* specifically, the decision-theoretic result is a warning: adding agent stages that merely reprocess shared context without new exogenous information cannot help and typically degrades reliability versus one agent with the same evidence (centralized .907 vs 3-agent relay .435, 5-agent .225) [Reliability]. The format of shared state matters more than its length — a typed posterior-vector relay loses far less per stage than free-form prose (75.2% vs 58.1% at 3 stages) — but, per the [mixed] correction, that is a *structure* win at a *smaller* budget, not an equal-length comparison [Reliability].

**Net design recommendation for ARP/AIL-0.** Build the adapter layer with high confidence: it is the engineering answer to a robustly measured portability gap, and it should be a bidirectional schema↔target translator with a built-in output validator/repair step. Build the register discipline as *typed, publicly-checkable handoff packets with a complete act vocabulary* — also well-supported, with KQML's failure as the cautionary boundary (verifiable semantics, no dialect drift). But scope the most ambitious claim honestly: a *designed shared compressed IR* that beats no-IR at fan-out/swarm scale is **not yet demonstrated anywhere in the located evidence**. Position AIL-0's swarm-IR as a falsifiable hypothesis with a pre-specified ablation (IR-present vs IR-absent, fixed everything else), not as a settled benefit.

## 3. Per-Strand Evidence

### Strand A — Adapter Portability: do conventions port across providers?

**[supported] Optimized prompts do not transfer; per-target re-adaptation is needed.** PromptBridge: the GPT-5–optimal HumanEval prompt scores 99.39% on GPT-5 but 68.70% transferred to Llama-3.1-70B-Instruct, vs Llama's own optimum 79.47% (10.77pp "model drifting" gap). PromptBridge is a training-free re-adaptation framework; recovery examples include CodeContests GPT-4o→o3 48.61%→56.36% (+7.75pp absolute) and SWE-Bench Verified o4-mini→o3 33.40%→46.00% (the paper's "+27.39%" is correctly labeled *relative*). These are measured pass@ accuracies. *Caveat (framing):* the ARP-adapter conclusion is design inference; the paper does not mention ARP. [PromptBridge]

**[supported] The optimal text/format convention is model-specific.** Sclar et al. (FormatSpread, ICLR 2024): semantically-equivalent format changes (delimiters/casing/spacing/separators) produce performance differences up to 76 accuracy points on LLaMA-2-13B, and "format performance only weakly correlates between models." This directly undermines "one canonical prompt format for all providers." *Caveat (framing):* the per-target-adapter conclusion is inference; the weak-correlation finding is solid. [FormatSpread]

**[supported] Output-format conventions collapse differently across families.** PromptPort (37,346 test examples, six open-weight families): 0.4–0.6 F1 portability gaps; format-recovery +6–8 F1, verifier-driven selection +14–16 F1, final Safe-Override 0.890 = 99.3% of zero-shot oracle 0.896; DistilBERT (66M) verifier. *Caveats:* (1) single-author preprint, low external validation; (2) the Gemma-2B ROS 0.116/CSS 0.246 exemplar **overstates** "format collapse" — Gemma-2B is weak in absolute terms regardless of format, and only ~0.13 of the failure is format-recoverable; the portability-gap finding is sound but the chosen exemplar is the weakest case. [PromptPort]

**[supported] Instruction-following reliability under paraphrase varies sharply by family.** IFEval++ (20 proprietary + 26 open-source models): relative decline from standard accuracy to reliable@10 (k=10) ranges 18.3% (GPT-5, "the most reliable model") to 61.8% (Qwen3-0.6B); "current LLMs often exhibit considerable inconsistent competence across cousin prompts." *Caveat (framing):* the "adapter cannot assume a strong-model convention holds on a weak model" conclusion is inference; the reliability-variance finding is solid. [IFEval++]

**[mixed] Complete up-front constraints are followed better than partial/late ones — but on thin evidence.** Single-block delivery scored S-0 2.4/10, S-1 4.1–6.0/10, S-2 (unified system block) 6.3/10. The study varied *completeness/delivery*, **not** system-vs-user position (no positional A/B). Marked mixed on **evidence quality**: self-published blog, n=1 per model/version, no repeats or error bars. Directionally plausible; thin support. [AIMuse]

**[mixed] No model achieves full hard-constraint compliance; absolute compliance is family-dependent.** 0 perfect 10/10 runs; name-quota violations 89%, banned-lexeme 94%, irreversible-loss 56%; top GPT-4(o1) 7/10, o3 6/10, Claude-Sonnet 6/10; lower Mistral-Medium 4/10, Gemma-27B 3/10, Grok-3 2/10. Mixed on **evidence quality** (same blog, n=1): the qualitative finding (strict compliance is probabilistic and family-dependent) is well-supported; the precise per-model ordering is within single-sample noise. [AIMuse]

**[thin] System-prompt adherence *style* differs by family (Claude more literal/rigid, GPT more self-substituting).** Practitioner observation, single example (TESTCASE-3), "from our experience" — no controlled benchmark, no numbers. Appropriately hedged; should be validated. Note the cross-check: on the AIMuse task GPT-4 (7/10) outscored Claude-Sonnet (6/10), so "rigidity" and raw compliance are distinct dimensions. [Coxit]

**Strand verdict:** Strongly supported that conventions do not port. The portability gap is measured across four independent studies; the per-target-adapter response is design inference, not paper claim.

### Strand B — Agent-Communication Languages: what register discipline did KQML/FIPA-ACL teach?

**[supported] Speech-act ACLs failed because their semantics were mentalistic and unverifiable.** Singh 1998: "both Arcol and KQML emphasize the private perspective... concerned only with the sender's perspective"; "If you cannot determine compliance, the standard is useless. Furthermore, for compliance to be testable the ACL's semantics must have a public perspective." KQML's `(tell :sender A :receiver B :content raining)` presupposes a private virtual knowledge base; Singh proposes social-commitment / public-perspective semantics as the fix. [Singh1998]

**[supported] KQML failed in practice via dialect proliferation.** "Although ACLs are being used in proprietary multiagent applications, nonproprietary agents cannot interoperate"; "Past efforts to standardize on the Knowledge Query Management Language... failed because many dialects arose"; "KQML was designed for interoperation (although it failed)." [Singh1998]

**[supported] ACL performatives had limited speech-act coverage.** "Arcol and KQML have limited coverage; all primitives are either assertives or directives. In Arcol, you can simulate commissives using other acts." Singh argues all seven categories (assertives, directives, commissives, permissives, prohibitives, declaratives, expressives) are needed: "Commissives are essential... Declaratives aid in appointing an agent as a representative." [Singh1998]

**[supported] MetaGPT constrains inter-agent messages to structured outputs over a shared pool.** Role-specific structured outputs (PRDs, design docs, interface specs, diagrams) exchanged via shared publish-subscribe message pool rather than free dialogue; "the use of intermediate structured outputs significantly increases the success rate of target code generation." Reaches 85.9% Pass@1 on HumanEval, 87.7% on MBPP (mapping confirmed: 85.9% = HumanEval, 87.7% = MBPP). Measured Pass@1. [MetaGPT]

**[mixed] MetaGPT's executable-feedback loop helps, but the "100% task completion" headline is mis-glossed.** Confirmed: +4.2pp Pass@1 (HumanEval), +5.4pp (MBPP), max 3 retries; SoftwareDev executability 3.67→3.75, human revision cost 2.25→0.83. **Defect:** the paper states "100% task completion rate" but never defines it; executability is a *separate* 1–4 metric on which MetaGPT scores 3.75 (not 100%). The gloss that "100%" *was* executability is unsupported — do not relabel it. Numbers solid, gloss unsupported. [MetaGPT]

**[mixed] MAST shows inter-agent misalignment is a top-level failure category.** 1,642 annotated traces, 7 frameworks, 14 modes in 3 categories, taxonomy from 150 traces, κ=0.88, failure rate 41%–86.7%. Component modes: FM-2.6 reasoning-action mismatch 13.2%, FM-2.3 task derailment 7.40%, FM-2.2 wrong assumptions 6.80%, FM-2.1 conversation reset 2.20%, FM-2.5 ignoring others' input 1.90%, FM-2.4 withholding info 0.85%. **Defect:** the claim that "no category-level split exists in the text" is false — Figure 1 gives category totals (31.41% / 37.17% / 31.41%), inter-agent misalignment ≈31.41%. Per-mode figures verbatim; one false sub-statement. [MAST]

**[supported] MAST concludes message-schema structuring is necessary but not sufficient.** Insight 2: "Solutions focused on context or communication protocols are often insufficient for FC2 failures, which demand deeper social reasoning." ChatDev CEO-final-say fix: +9.4% task success — but "achieving robust MAS reliability often requires more than isolated fixes... fundamental MAS redesigns." Bounds what structuring alone buys. [MAST]

**Strand verdict:** Strongly supported. The KQML/FIPA lesson — verifiable, public-perspective semantics with a complete act vocabulary and no dialect drift — is a direct design constraint. Modern frameworks confirm structured messaging helps but cap its sufficiency.

### Strand C — Register Discipline: does separating channels / typing handoffs help?

**[supported] Channel separation cuts tokens ~5x (ReWOO).** Planner-Worker-Solver split detaches reasoning from observations so tool outputs and prior context are not re-fed each step: ReAct 9,795.1 → ReWOO 1,986.2 measured tokens on HotpotQA (4.93x, rounded to "5x"). Head-to-head baseline (interleaved ReAct); the register split is the design delta. The cleanest causal claim in the dossier. [ReWOO]

**[supported] Channel separation also slightly improves accuracy (ReWOO).** HotpotQA 42.4% vs ReAct 40.8% = +1.6pp absolute = +3.9% relative. The abstract's "4%" is *relative*, not 4pp — reading it as +4pp would overstate. Real but small. [ReWOO]

**[supported, asserted-not-demonstrated] Structured-artifact handoffs reduce cross-handoff information loss (MetaGPT).** Agents "communicate through documents and diagrams (structured outputs) rather than dialogue"; "these documents contain all necessary information, preventing irrelevant or missing content." This is the paper's design *rationale*; no ablation isolates structured-handoff vs free-form dialogue. Faithful to the source's stated rationale, not a demonstrated causal effect. [MetaGPT]

**[supported, system-level] Role-specialized channels + structured pub-sub schemas + critique/test role yield high code-gen success (MetaGPT).** HumanEval 85.9%, MBPP 87.7%, roles = Product Manager/Architect/Project Manager/Engineer/QA. System-level results; they do **not** isolate the causal effect of channel separation alone. [MetaGPT]

**[supported] An executable-feedback/verification channel measurably raises accuracy (MetaGPT, isolated ablation).** "Adding executable feedback... leads to a significant improvement of 4.2% and 5.4% in Pass@1 on HumanEval and MBPP." Verified as percentage *points* (baseline ~81.7/82.3 → 85.9/87.7). The cleanest isolated numeric of the MetaGPT claims. [MetaGPT]

**[supported] Inter-agent handoffs are dominated by a small set of structured failure modes (AgentAsk).** 824 logs, agent-to-agent message as unit: Signal Corruption 36.8%, Data Gap 29.1%, Referential Drift 27.3%, Capability Gap 6.8% (sum 100%, clean partition). The two largest (corruption + gaps = 65.9%) are exactly what validated handoff packets address. [AgentAsk]

**[supported] A lightweight clarification controller at handoffs improves accuracy at near-zero overhead (AgentAsk).** Mean ~+0.5pp across ~17/20 cells, <5% latency and <5% token overhead. MasRouter@GSM8K: 93.26% → 94.72% (+1.46pp) at 103% latency / 4.2% extra cost, vs a heavier GPT-5 evaluator (+2.08pp but 134% latency / 38% cost). Modest but real; AgentAsk gets most of the gain at a fraction of the cost. [AgentAsk]

**Strand verdict:** Supported. Register/channel discipline (separation, typed artifacts, verification channels, handoff clarification) measurably helps cost and reliability. Most MetaGPT numbers are system-level, not isolations; ReWOO and the executable-feedback ablation are the clean causal results.

### Strand D — Multi-Agent State Compression: does shared structured-state compression help cost/reliability?

**[supported] Reprocessing shared context without new information cannot help and typically degrades reliability.** Common-evidence relay (downstream stages see only the relayed message): gpt-4.1-mini centralized .907 vs 2-agent .412, 3-agent .435, 5-agent .225 (below 25% chance); o4-mini .899 → .370. Decision-theoretic dominance (Proposition 6): a delegated network is dominated by a centralized Bayes decision-maker observing the same exogenous evidence. Fully supported and foundational — a warning against gratuitous stages. [Reliability]

**[mixed] Interface *format* of shared state matters more than length — but the "equal-length" framing is wrong.** Typed posterior-vector relay vs free-form prose: 75.2% vs 58.1% at 3 stages; −2.8 vs −8.5 pts/stage (n=750, 50-Q MMLU). The thesis (posterior distortion, not transcript length, is the real object) is the paper's Remark 4 and fully supported. **But** the "equal-length / same-budget" qualifier is **contradicted**: both conditions fix *stage count*, not length — prose got "2–3 paragraphs," posterior got "ONLY four numbers." It is a structure win at a *far smaller* budget, not an equal one. [Reliability]

**[mixed] Structured compression cuts peak token footprint ~26–54% — but the accuracy claim is overstated (ACON).** Peak-token framing correct: AppWorld 9.93K→7.33K (26.1%), OfficeBench 7.27K→4.54K (37.5%), Multi-obj QA 10.35K→4.65K (55.1%). AppWorld accuracy 56.0%→56.5%, OfficeBench 76.84%→72.63%. **Refuted sub-claim:** "matching/exceeding EM/F1" on Multi-obj QA is false — ACON EM 0.335/F1 0.458 vs uncompressed 0.366/0.488 (drops ~6–8%), and ACON is beaten by a plain Prompting baseline (EM 0.376). ACON beats *compression* baselines (FIFO/Retrieval/LLMLingua), not uncompressed. Token reduction holds; the accuracy claim does not. [ACON]

**[supported] Compression can be distilled into a small dedicated compressor, decoupling cost (ACON).** Distilled compressors (Qwen3-14B/8B, Phi-4) retain >95% of the gpt-4.1 teacher-compressor's accuracy across all three benchmarks; ACON-compressed context improves smaller LMs as agents up to ~46% (AppWorld Qwen3-14B 26.8%→33.9%). *Arithmetic note:* 26.8→33.9 = +26.5% relative, not the parenthetical "~+32%" (non-load-bearing). [ACON]

**[supported] A blackboard/shared-scratchpad pattern beats RAG and master-slave orchestration on reliability.** Gemini 2.5 Pro: KramaBench 28.26/32.16/37.53, DSBench 27.27/34.38/38.73, DA-Code 0/5.49/9.34 — blackboard wins all three; 13–57% relative success improvement plus up to 9% relative F1 discovery gain. Honestly framed as a *state-sharing reliability* result, not token savings: no total token-cost comparison reported (only an 8,192 tok/step cap, max 10 actions). [Blackboard]

**[supported] Blackboard reliability hinges on writing subordinate responses to a *separate* response board.** "Responses are not written back to the blackboard... to avoid dependencies where one sub-agent's output could influence the behavior of others negatively. Instead, all responses are directed exclusively to the response board." Helper agents self-select participation; no central task assignment. A concrete anti-contamination design constraint. [Blackboard]

**[supported] Runtime pruning of redundant inter-agent + tool-output content yields ~19–39% token reduction holding/improving success — but inter-agent savings are NOT isolated.** GAIA 29.68%, HumanEval 23.74% (92.07%→92.68%), AIME 18.92% (+6.67% solve rate), AWorld 36.54%, OAgents 39.36%. **Crucial caveat (confirmed):** the paper bundles three strategies and does not separate inter-agent-message savings from tool-output/memory savings; the ablation shows tool-output purification is the dominant lever (disabling it drops savings 29.68%→15.96%). So the savings are mostly *tool-output*, not inter-agent communication. [Pruning]

**[mixed] Production frameworks converge on structured handoffs + tool-aware compaction — but two specifics are wrong.** The convergence thesis (structured packets, dedup/prune/summarize old tool outputs, protect head/tail, iteratively update prior summary) partly holds. **But:** (1) Hermes is documented as a *single-layer* head-middle-tail system; the "two-layer 85%/50%" thresholds are **unsupported** by the doc. (2) Morph reports controlled-ish numbers (SWE-Bench Pro 3–4x fewer compactions, 98% verbatim accuracy, 37% multi-session retention, 50–70% compression, 36,000-message Factory.ai eval), so "design descriptions without falsifiable numbers" is **false for Morph** (true for LangChain). [Hermes][LangChain][Morph]

**Strand verdict:** Mixed-leaning-supported for *handoff/state discipline*; the format of state matters and structured compression saves tokens. But the strongest reliability results (blackboard) are not token results, the strongest token results (ACON, Pruning) do not cleanly help end-accuracy or do not isolate inter-agent savings, and gratuitous stages actively hurt. Real but qualified.

### Strand E — Shared IR / Register Language at Swarm Scale: the central null result

**[supported] No located source isolates "designed shared compressed IR vs none" at fan-out/swarm scale with head-to-head cost/reliability.** Strong numbers come from adjacent levers (learned NL compression, sparsification, transport choice, shared log); the designed-register-IR construct is largely proposal-stage (e.g. LACP is "argumentative rather than empirical," no experiments). **This null result is the finding.** Scope: "no source located within the searches performed," not universal nonexistence. Nuance: ANP is actually implemented/benchmarked in ProtocolBench, but ProtocolBench varies *protocol choice*, not IR-presence. [LACP][ProtocolBench]

**[mixed — accurate claim, adjacent evidence] OPTIMA cuts tokens up to 88.5% preserving accuracy — but 2-agent scale, learned NL compression, not a designed IR.** "GSM8k-trained iDPO matches CoT-SC performance with 88.5% fewer tokens"; "focusing on two-agent scenarios without external tools"; mechanism = multi-objective reward (task + token + readability) on Llama 3 8B. Genuine token reduction; weak/adjacent evidence for swarm-scale shared-IR. [OPTIMA]

**[mixed — adjacent] OPTIMA: 2WikiMultihopQA F1 74.2 vs 25.9 for Multi-Agent Debate (2.86x) using ~10% of tokens (54.9 vs 543.7), 2-agent Llama 3 8B.** Numbers exact; still 2-agent, still learned NL compression, not an IR. [OPTIMA]

**[mixed — adjacent] S2-MAD cuts MAD token cost up to 94.5% with <2% accuracy degradation — via sparsification, not a shared representation.** GSM8K 20.4k→4.53k; some tasks improve (MMLU 74.1%→76.8%); mechanism = Conditional Participation Module (redundancy filtering), 5–8 agents. Fan-out-relevant but the variable is sparsity, not IR. [S2-MAD]

**[mixed — adjacent] Communication protocol/transport choice measurably affects cost/reliability.** Completion time varies up to 36.5%, mean end-to-end latency by 3.48s across protocols (latency metrics, not token savings); "protocol choice significantly influences system behavior." Evidences that the adapter/protocol layer matters — *orthogonal* to whether a compressed IR helps. [ProtocolBench]

**[mixed — adjacent] ProtocolRouter reduces Fail-Storm recovery time up to 18.1%; adaptive protocol selection improves resilience.** The 18.1% is verified; the absolutes (8.00s→6.55s), GAIA figures, and Fail-Storm scenario specifics (8-agent Shard-QA ring, 3/8 killed/120s) are **unverified** — not on the primary page. Variable is protocol selection, not IR. [ProtocolBench]

**[thin] LogAct shared typed append-only log (AgentBus) is the closest on-strand positive — but confounded.** 6-agent swarm, supervisor introspecting peers' logs did "17% more work... 41% fewer tokens." AgentBus = "linearizable, durable shared log" with typed entries (Intention/Vote/Commit/Abort/Result/Mail/Policy). **Confound:** Base (mailbox-only) vs Supervisor (introspection added) is an introspection-on/off comparison with no external non-LogAct baseline — does not isolate the shared-log representation. Single source. [LogAct]

**[thin] LogAct reliability/safety gains — but misattributed.** Dual-voter setup: 1.4% attack success at 78.4% benign utility (vs 49.5% rule-based-alone); logging overhead 2.6KB/s. **Attribution overstatement:** this isolates *dual-voter vs rule-based-alone* (a voter-policy comparison on AgentDojo), **not** shared-log-substrate vs none. The headline result does not measure the shared-IR construct it is cited for. [LogAct]

**[supported] Structured/typed cross-agent schemas exist and coincide with gains, but no source isolates structured-grammar vs free-form NL at swarm scale.** LogAct typed entries and ProtocolBench schemas both report cost/reliability differences; neither runs a grammar-vs-freeform ablation. Careful, hedged null finding. [LogAct][ProtocolBench]

**Strand verdict:** The most important honest finding in the report. Adjacent levers (NL compression, sparsification, transport, shared log) show large gains, but **not one isolates the designed-shared-IR construct**. AIL-0's swarm-IR benefit is a hypothesis, not a measured result.

## 4. Design Implications for ARP / AIL-0 (Multi-Agent)

1. **Build the adapter as a bidirectional schema↔target translator, not a one-way emitter.** Conventions do not port (10.77pp HumanEval transfer loss [PromptBridge]; up to 76 accuracy points from format alone, weak cross-model correlation [FormatSpread]; 0.4–0.6 F1 output-format gaps [PromptPort]). The adapter must render the canonical register into each target's tolerated convention *and* canonicalize/repair the target's output.

2. **Include a verifier/repair step in the adapter.** PromptPort: format-recovery +6–8 F1, verifier-driven selection +14–16 F1, reaching 99.3% of oracle [PromptPort]; a 66M DistilBERT verifier sufficed — the repair layer can be cheap.

3. **Do not assume a convention validated on a strong model holds on a weak one.** Reliability declines 18.3%→61.8% across families under convention-preserving paraphrase [IFEval++]; no model achieves full hard-constraint compliance and compliance is family-dependent [AIMuse]. Adapter conventions must be validated per target, weakest targets included.

4. **Make register semantics publicly checkable, defined over observable artifacts — never over agent beliefs/intent.** Singh's principle: "if you cannot determine compliance, the standard is useless... compliance must have a public perspective" [Singh1998]. ARP register entries should be defined over what is written to shared state, third-party verifiable.

5. **Tightly specify the schema to prevent dialect drift.** KQML fragmented into incompatible dialects so "nonproprietary agents cannot interoperate" [Singh1998] — the concrete historical failure ARP must avoid via a versioned, conformance-tested schema.

6. **Define a complete, first-class act/intent vocabulary.** KQML/Arcol covered only assertives/directives; commissives and declaratives were simulated inconsistently [Singh1998]. AIL-0's register kinds should include commissive (commitments) and declarative (role/authority changes) as primitives.

7. **Prefer typed, structured handoff packets over free-form messages — with the caveat that structure is necessary, not sufficient.** Structured artifacts carry "all necessary information, preventing missing content" [MetaGPT]; AgentAsk shows handoff failures are dominated by Signal Corruption (36.8%) + Data Gap (29.1%) [AgentAsk] — exactly what validated packets address. But MAST: schemas alone leave inter-agent misalignment unsolved; targeted fixes give only +9.4% [MAST].

8. **Separate reasoning, observation, and tool-output into distinct register channels.** ReWOO's split cut tokens ~4.93x ("5x") with a small accuracy gain on HotpotQA by not re-feeding observations each step [ReWOO] — the cleanest causal result for register discipline.

9. **Provide a first-class verification/test channel.** MetaGPT's executable-feedback ablation: +4.2pp / +5.4pp Pass@1 [MetaGPT] — an isolated, positive result for a verification register kind.

10. **Add a lightweight handoff-clarification controller at register edges.** AgentAsk: ~+0.5pp mean, up to +1.46pp (MasRouter@GSM8K) at <5% latency/token overhead [AgentAsk] — cheap error-propagation reduction.

11. **Do not add agent stages that merely reprocess shared state.** Decision-theoretic dominance: relay networks without new exogenous information are dominated by a centralized decision-maker and degrade badly (.907 → .435 → .225) [Reliability]. ARP should require each stage to add exogenous evidence.

12. **Compress shared state by structure, not just length — and measure peak tokens.** Typed posterior relay beats free-form prose per stage [Reliability]; structured compaction cuts peak tokens ~26–54% [ACON]. *But* do not promise accuracy parity: ACON drops ~6–8% on Multi-obj QA vs uncompressed [ACON], and the posterior-relay win was at a *smaller* budget, not equal-length [Reliability].

13. **Optionally distill the compressor into a small dedicated model.** ACON distilled compressors retain >95% of teacher accuracy and lift smaller agent LMs up to ~46% [ACON] — decouples compression cost from the main agent.

14. **Use a blackboard with a separate response board to prevent cross-agent contamination.** Blackboard beat RAG and master-slave on all three benchmarks (13–57% relative gain) [Blackboard]; the key design choice is routing subordinate responses to a *separate* board, not back onto the shared state [Blackboard].

15. **Scope AIL-0's swarm-IR benefit as a falsifiable hypothesis, with a pre-registered IR-present-vs-absent ablation.** No located source isolates a designed shared compressed IR vs none at swarm scale [LACP][ProtocolBench]; the strong swarm numbers belong to NL compression [OPTIMA], sparsification [S2-MAD], transport [ProtocolBench], and shared logs [LogAct] — none of which is the IR construct. ARP should ship the ablation it would take to actually prove the claim.

## 5. Open Questions / Thin Evidence

- **The core swarm-IR claim is unproven.** No located source isolates designed-shared-IR-vs-none at fan-out scale with head-to-head cost/reliability [LACP][ProtocolBench]. LACP is a position paper with no experiments. The closest positives (LogAct) are single-source and confounded.
- **LogAct's two on-strand results are both attribution-confounded.** The 17%-more-work/41%-fewer-tokens result compares introspection on/off, not the shared-log representation; the 1.4% ASR / 78.4% utility result is a voter-policy comparison, not shared-log-vs-none [LogAct]. Single source, no external baseline.
- **Inter-agent vs tool-output token savings are never isolated.** The ~19–39% pruning savings are dominated by tool-output purification, not inter-agent communication [Pruning]; ACON's peak-token reductions also bundle observations + interaction history [ACON]. The specific value of compressing *inter-agent* messages is unquantified.
- **Family-level adherence *style* (literal vs self-substituting) is anecdotal only** — single example, no benchmark [Coxit]; contradicted in raw-compliance ranking by AIMuse (GPT-4 7/10 > Claude-Sonnet 6/10) [AIMuse]. Do not treat as a measured family ranking.
- **The hard-constraint compliance evidence is a non-peer-reviewed blog, n=1 per model** [AIMuse]; per-model orderings are within single-sample noise. The qualitative findings are plausible; the precise rankings are not bankable.
- **ProtocolRouter's Fail-Storm scenario details and absolute timings are unverified** — not on the primary page [ProtocolBench].
- **PromptPort is a single-author preprint with low external validation**, and its showcase exemplar (Gemma-2B) overstates format-recoverability [PromptPort].
- **MetaGPT's "100% task completion" is undefined in the paper** and should not be cited as executability or as binary success [MetaGPT].
- **No controlled grammar-vs-free-form NL ablation exists at swarm scale** [LogAct][ProtocolBench] — the benefit attributable specifically to *structure* (vs protocol, supervisor, or sparsity) is unmeasured.
