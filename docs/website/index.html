Why it's different

A system that audits itself — and can fail itself

Most dashboards assert trust. Vaked measures it. Integrity claims are checked by a verifier that lives outside the thing it verifies, anchored to a GPG-signed seal the repo cannot rewrite. When the swarm found its own instrumentation lying, it published the catch instead of hiding it — and built a gate that turns red on a falsehood.

Measured, not asserted
Trust derived from state; a number you can't reproduce is decoration.
Externally anchored
Seals signed with a key outside the repo; tamper-with-reseal fails verification.
Self-auditing
The gate was attacked six ways and hardened against each — in the open.
Append-only
Hash-chained event ledger; the record cannot be quietly rewritten.
